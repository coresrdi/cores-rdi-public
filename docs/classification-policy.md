# Classification Policy

## Overview

CORES RDI maintains a two-level document classification system to balance open collaboration with intellectual property protection.

---

## Level A — Public

**Repository:** [cores-rdi-public](https://github.com/coresrdi/cores-rdi-public)

Content available to anyone:

- Project overview and mission rationale
- Volume outlines and chapter titles
- General technical summaries (non-proprietary)
- Glossary and reference lists
- Contributing guidelines
- FAQ and general resources

### What qualifies as Level A?

- Information that provides context without revealing proprietary methods
- High-level descriptions of systems and approaches
- Publicly available scientific data and references
- General architecture diagrams (without detailed specifications)

---

## Level B — Confidential

**Repository:** [cores-rdi](https://github.com/coresrdi/cores-rdi) (private)

Restricted content for authorized contributors:

- Full chapter content with detailed technical analysis
- Proprietary calculations and engineering data
- Design decisions and trade study results
- Detailed system specifications
- Financial models and projections
- Internal project intelligence and research notes
- BookStack automation scripts and workflows

### What qualifies as Level B?

- Original engineering analysis and calculations
- Specific dimensions, pressures, temperatures, and performance data
- Cost estimates and financial projections
- Internal workflow documentation
- Unpublished research and design rationale

---

## Guidelines for Contributors

1. **When in doubt, classify as Level B** — it is easier to declassify than to retract
2. **Outlines are Level A**, full chapters are Level B
3. **General concepts are Level A**, specific numbers and calculations are Level B
4. **Published references are Level A**, original analysis of those references is Level B

---

## Requesting Access

To request access to Level B content, open an issue in the public repository or contact the project maintainers.
