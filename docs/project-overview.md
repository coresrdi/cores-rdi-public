# CORES RDI — Project Overview

## What is CORES?

**CORES** stands for **Colonization & Orbital Research for Excavation**. The Red Dawn Initiative (RDI) is the operational branch focused on developing a **Planetary Subterrane Tunneling System** for Mars.

The core concept: use autonomous Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) to create subsurface habitats on Mars, lined with locally-produced basalt glass for structural integrity, thermal insulation, and radiation shielding.

## Why Subsurface?

Mars surface conditions present major challenges for long-term habitation:

- Radiation exposure (no magnetic field, thin atmosphere)
- Temperature extremes (-60°C average)
- Dust storms and micrometeorite impacts
- Low atmospheric pressure (~600 Pa)

Subsurface tunnels provide natural protection against all of these hazards while enabling scalable, expandable habitat networks.

## Mission Architecture

The CORES approach is documented across 10 volumes covering:

1. **Foundation** — Vision, philosophy, mission rationale
2. **Science** — Mars environment data and site selection criteria
3. **Tunneling** — TBM design (Soulavin system), drilling operations
4. **Materials** — ISRU processes, basalt glass lining, construction methods
5. **Energy** — Power generation, storage, distribution networks
6. **Habitat** — Interior design, ECLSS, life support systems
7. **Logistics** — Starship supply chain, cargo manifests, ISRU transition
8. **Communications** — Networks, data systems, Earth-Mars relay
9. **Human Factors** — Crew selection, training, psychological support
10. **Finance** — Economic model, funding strategy, sustainability

## Key Technologies

- **Soulavin TBM System** — Custom tunnel boring machine adapted for Martian regolith
- **Basalt Glass Lining** — In-situ produced structural liner from local basalt rock
- **ISRU Pipeline** — Progressive transition from Earth-supplied to Mars-manufactured materials
- **Modular Habitat Segments** — Standardized tunnel sections for different functions

## Project Status

CORES RDI is in active development with content being produced across all 10 volumes. The project is open to collaboration from researchers, engineers, and space enthusiasts worldwide.
