# CORES RDI — Frequently Asked Questions

## General

**Q: What is CORES RDI?**
A: CORES (Colonization & Orbital Research for Excavation) Red Dawn Initiative is a research and engineering project developing a planetary tunneling system for Mars colonization using subsurface habitats.

**Q: Why underground?**
A: The Martian surface is hostile — high radiation, extreme temperatures, low pressure, and dust storms. Subsurface tunnels provide natural shielding and a stable environment for long-term habitation.

**Q: What is the Soulavin?**
A: The Soulavin is the CORES-designed Tunnel Boring Machine (TBM) adapted for Martian conditions. It is the core technology of the project.

## Technical

**Q: How does the basalt glass lining work?**
A: Martian basalt rock is processed in-situ to produce a glass lining that coats the interior of excavated tunnels, providing structural integrity, thermal insulation, and radiation shielding.

**Q: What power source does the colony use?**
A: The primary power source is nuclear fission (Kilopower/FSP technology). Solar is considered only for Phase 0 bootstrap. Advanced geothermal concepts are being explored as a long-term Track 2 option.

**Q: How big are the tunnels?**
A: The reference tunnel diameter is 9 meters, forming a primary loop network that serves as the colony backbone.

## Project

**Q: How can I contribute?**
A: See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for guidelines. You can review outlines, suggest improvements, report issues, or help with translations.

**Q: Why are there two repositories?**
A: The public repo contains outlines and general information (Level A). The private repo contains detailed technical content, calculations, and proprietary data (Level B). See the [Classification Policy](../docs/classification-policy.md).

**Q: What stage is the project at?**
A: CORES RDI is in active research and documentation phase, with content being developed across all 10 volumes. No hardware has been built yet.

**Q: Is this affiliated with SpaceX or NASA?**
A: No. CORES RDI is an independent project. However, the mission architecture assumes SpaceX Starship for transportation logistics.
